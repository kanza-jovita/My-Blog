My Two-Week Journey with Leadership & Personal Development.(The 7 Habits of highlt effective people)

In this blog post, I am sharing my transformative experience from a leadership and personal development class. Initially expecting to learn about leading large teams or nations, I found the course profoundly impactful on a personal level. It introduced me to the seven principles of highly effective people, teaching me to identify strengths and weaknesses, value diversity, plan effectively, and build meaningful relationships. Thanks to my facilitator, Julia, I now see life from a new perspective, reigniting my passion for personal growth. With newfound tools, I'm excited to continue learning and become a highly effective leader.

Autobiographical listening

I learnt that autobiographical listening is a powerful tool for self-reflection and personal growth. By listening to the life stories of others, you can gain valuable insights into your own beliefs, values, biases, and assumptions. Through this introspection, you can develop a deeper understanding of yourself and how your experiences shape your view of the world. So, embrace the opportunity to listen and learn from others, and let their stories inspire you on your own journey of self-discovery.


Principles of effectiveness

The path to success is paved with clear and achievable goals. That's why the principles of effectiveness stress the importance of goal-setting. By adopting the SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) framework, you can set goals that are well-defined, quantifiable, practical, aligned to your objectives, and time-bound. This approach enables you to channel your efforts, prioritize your tasks, and measure your progress effectively, ensuring that you're always moving forward. By mastering these principles, you can unlock your full potential, stay motivated, and achieve your goals with confidence.

Think win-win

Think win-win" means that you should seek mutual benefit in any interaction or negotiation. Instead of treating it as a zero-sum game where one person's gain is another's loss, try to find solutions that meet the needs and interests of all parties involved. This way, you can create value and benefit for everyone involved.


Highly effective practices

I learned that focusing on my circle of influence teaches me to be proactive, empowered, and resilient in navigating life's challenges and pursuing my goals. It encourages a mindset of personal responsibility and agency, leading to greater fulfillment, effectiveness, and well-being.
